ALS Offers

"ALS opens doors to new beginnings—a second chance for dreamers who once had to pause their journey in school, but never gave up on learning."

Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E):

This program allows individuals to complete elementary and high school education outside the traditional classroom setting.

Basic Literacy Program (BLP):

This program focuses on developing essential literacy skills like reading, writing, and numeracy.

Community-Based Learning:

ALS often emphasizes community-based learning, promoting self-help and community development.

ALS for Persons Deprived of Liberty (PDL)

ALS learning programs offered inside jails and correctional facilities to help persons deprived of liberty gain education and skills for reintegration into society.

ALS for Children in Conflict with the Law (CICL)

A specialized ALS program for minors who are in conflict with the law, providing education while they are under the care of youth centers or rehabilitation facilities.

Abot-Alam Program

A partnership-driven ALS initiative that maps out-of-school youth aged 15–30 and provides them with appropriate interventions such as education, skills training, or employment opportunities.

Community ALS Implementers

Individuals engaged by DepEd or LGUs to deliver ALS programs
